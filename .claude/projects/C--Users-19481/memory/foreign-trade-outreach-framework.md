## Foreign Trade B2B Outreach — Complete Framework

### 1. The Golden Framework: Why You — Why Me — Why Now

```
Why You : I noticed [specific signal about their company/market]
Why Me  : We helped [similar client] achieve [specific result] in [market]
Why Now : [Light CTA] Worth 15 min to see how?
```

This replaces the traditional "We are a leading manufacturer..." opening. Lead with THEM, not you.

### 2. Three-Section Cold Email (for batch sending)

| Section | Function | Length |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Value Hook | Position their industry + specific observation + value proposition | 1-2 sentences |
| Benefit Breakdown | Reduce what cost? Improve what efficiency? Deliver what certainty? | 2-3 bullets |
| Clear CTA | Light, specific, executable. Single-action. Low pressure. | 1 sentence |

### 3. Follow-Up Cadence: 7-3-7 Model

```
Day 0    → First email (open + soft value hook)
Day 2-4  → Follow-up 1 (different angle, new value piece)
Day 6-8  → Follow-up 2 (case study or industry data)
Day 10-14 → Follow-up 3 (light touch, exit option)
```

**Critical stat**: 80% of B2B orders come from touches 5-10. Most give up after 1-2.

### 4. Regional Communication Styles

| Market | Style | Strategy |
|--------|-------|----------|
| North America | Direct, data-driven | Open strong + numbers + clear CTA |
| Europe (DE/UK) | Rigorous, logical | Certifications + technical depth + trust building |
| Middle East | Relationship-first | Formal greeting + long-term partnership + patience |
| India | Price-sensitive | Value-for-money + WhatsApp + flexible MOQ |
| Japan/Korea | Humble, polite | Modest language + quality-first + no hard sell |
| MENA (specific) | Honor + relationship | "We want to grow with you" framing, not transactional |

### 5. ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) Modeling

Before any outreach, define 3 ICP personas:
```
ICP-A: [Role] at [Company Type] in [Region] — cares about [pain 1], [pain 2]
ICP-B: ...
ICP-C: ...
```

### 6. Multi-Channel Synergy Matrix

```
Email      → First touch (personalized + value positioning)
LinkedIn   → Trust incubator (engage content + DM after connection)
Website    → Trust validator (case studies + certifications + process)
WhatsApp   → Real-time follow-up (India, Middle East, SE Asia)
Content    → Long-term gravity (SEO articles, case studies, videos)
```

### 7. Anti-Patterns That Kill Deliverability

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Spam words: Free, Cheap, Best Price, Promotion | Instant spam folder |
| Template without any personalization | Zero reply rate in 2026 |
| Attachment on first email | Blacklisted |
| "Dear Sir/Madam" | Tells them it's a blast |
| Multiple images/links in first email | Spam score skyrockets |
| Over-promising ("We can reduce your cost by 50%") | Kills trust instantly |

### 8. Key Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Open rate | 40-60% |
| Reply rate (signal-based) | 15-25% |
| Reply rate (generic) | 1-3% |
| B2B platform cost per inquiry | $50-80 |
| Social media cost per lead | Under $20 |
| LTV:CAC ratio | > 3:1 |
| Follow-ups per prospect | 5 minimum |

### 10. The 5-3-2 Resource Allocation Rule

| Budget % | Channel Type |
|----------|-------------|
| 50% | Stable high-efficiency: B2B platforms (Alibaba, MIC) |
| 30% | Emerging/testing: TikTok Shop, live factory tours, LinkedIn |
| 20% | Long-tail: industry directories, customs data tracking |

### 11. Summary: The 7 Commandments

1. ICP first, then prospect list — 80% energy on the right people
2. Research before every send — "Why them" replaces "Who I am"
3. Structured framework — Why You → Why Me → Why Now
4. CTA = light + specific — they should reply with one word
5. Sequence, don't blast — 5+ touches before expecting results
6. Multi-channel — email + LinkedIn + site = trust loop
7. Data-driven — track open rate, reply rate, A/B test subject lines
