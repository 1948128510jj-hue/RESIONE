## Regional Tone Adaptation

Same 5-layer framework (who → what they're proud of → what external factor breaks it → ask if they've seen it → what I do), different tone per region.

### Regional Rules

| Region | Tone | Key Tactics |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **North America** | Direct, data-driven, confident | Open with achievement, hit pain point directly, no fluff |
| **Middle East** | Respect, relationship, honor | Acknowledge reputation first, "grow together" framing, never transactional |
| **Pakistan/India** | Skill-respect, price-conscious | Praise technical ability, hint at value-for-money, WhatsApp preferred |
| **Southeast Asia** | Climate = natural pain point | Lock onto humidity/environment, production consistency |
| **Latin America** | Warm, personal, relationship | Use full name, warmer tone, "love what you're doing" |
| **Europe (DE/NL)** | Precise, technical, no fluff | Talk specs directly, don't flatter, they respect data |
| **East Asia (JP/KR/CN)** | Humble, indirect, trust-building | Use "our team" not "I", provide info without pushing, longer warm-up |
| **Africa** | Tech-curious, growth-focused | Respect skill, frame as helping them grow faster |

### Tone Distance (How Much Respect Buffer Needed)

US = 0m | Pakistan = 0.5m | Middle East = 2m | Japan = 5m

### Same Question, Different Tone

"What resin are you running?" →

- US: "What resin are you currently printing with?"
- Pakistan: "What resin are you running these days? Curious what's working."
- Middle East: "If I may ask — what materials has your lab chosen to work with?"
- Japan: "If it's not too much trouble to share — may I ask what resin your team prefers?"
